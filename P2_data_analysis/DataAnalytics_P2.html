  1. Replace NaN with average of all ages

Exploration points: From above results, when we divide the full data based on "family size column", we can see some normal and some weird trends. Like maximum people were travelling alone and people with very large families are very less. The weird finding is that number of people travelling alone have less survival rate as compared to people travelling with small families. The survival rate of single pasenger is just 30%, which is against the natural thought that single person can get adjusted any where and save themselves. But here the trends shows that people with family size of 2,3 and 4 were safest.

Also the large size families were not safe. Families with size greater then 4, were having survival rate even less then 35%.

The reason could be that family will have females and children, so they were given preference over single people. But for larger family it would have been hard to adjust so many people together because they all wanted to go together.

Step4: Conclusion

We can see above that passengers travelling alone were having less chances of survival as compared to passengers with family of size upto 4. Trends in the above results are clear that people travelling with family were safe in titanic as compared to people travelling alone, but if family size goes beyond 5 or more then they were not safe. The reason behind it could be that family were given preferences over single people. Also larger families with size above 5 would have taken more space thats why they had low survival rate. There is a corelation between small family size and survival.

Females are given preferences over male by a large margin.

Having money can save you and your families life, as we can see people who took cabins were having higher chances of survival, reason could they might have bribed the people to get onto the lifeboat. There is a corelation between survival and how rich you are.

These corelation doesn't leads to causation, as the actual situation at that moment would be very difficult and different, as when it comes to survival people tends to do anything. But these findings can make us guess what was the scenario at that moment and what someone could have done to increase the chances of survival.

In this exploration I have measured 1 dependent variable 'Survived' and 4 independent variable 'Familysize' (comprises of Parch and Sibsb), Gender, Cabin and Pclass.
